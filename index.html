About devOutpost

Utilize your network

Recruitment is a multi-million dollar industry. Chances are that your landed your current contract via one of the many recruitment agencies out there. devOutpost wants to cut out the middle man and have you work directly for your new client.

  • Set your search criteria with precision
  • Invoice your client directly
  • Get introduced to potential clients
  • Get intel from your fellow contractors
  • Free - there is no catch (see FAQ)

Why?

devOutpost was created in a response to the market rates of recruitment agencies. A typical contract landed via a recruiter adds between €10 and €20 per hour on top your hourly rate. Typical yearly revenue for a recruiting agency on single contract therefore is between €16,000 and €32,000 per year.

At devOutpost, we believe that contractors should be able to find contracts without a recruitment agency as middle man. Now, imagine that you have a central location where all contractors share

  • their experience at a certain company
  • potential new upcoming contracts
  • contractor recommendations

Then it would become much easier for clients to get in touch with contractors directly. And besides, who better to determine who is best qualified than contractors in your network?

How?

devOutpost is a network based on recommendations. You can only join devOutpost by invite of an existing devOutpost member. Once invited, you can provide us your criteria for contracts in a very concise and detailed manner:

  • Only considering taking a contract in Amsterdam if the rate is higher than €95?
  • Is the assignment within 20km of your home town? Then you are willing to accept €85;
  • Want to work in a particular sector? Sure.
  • Want to work for smaller or bigger scale companies?

And many more options. We got you covered.

Once you've signed up and have received some recommendations, you are on your way to find potential new contracts. Matching of contractors to contracts always works based on your network. You are only eligible for a contract if someone in your network (in)directly has recommended you for similar criteria. The bigger your network, the more chances you will have of finding a new contract.

Frequently Ask Questions

Why do I have to be invited?

Quality of quantity. We are only interested in having IT contractors in our network.

Can I invite anyone?

Yes you can. But we urge to you only invite IT contractors to this network. We have some systems in place to verify members. Non-IT-contractors may be blocked at any time.

How many people can I invite?

Every member initially gets 10 invitation codes.

Who should I recommend?

This is completely up to you. Everyone knows that one contractor they've worked with in the past and would definitely want to work with them again.

Is this only for developers?

No it is not. Anyone working professionally as a contractor in the IT field is welcome in this network. It doesn't matter if you are a developer, designer, architect, tester, in operations or have some other cool title. You work in IT as a contractor in the Netherlands? Be welcome!

Who can read my recommendations?

Recommendations are only used in the system to help better find suitable candidates for certain contracts. Recommendations are not shared with the person being recommended. In the future recommendations might be shared in an anonymous fashion, but for now they are kept privately.

What is the difference with LinkedIn?

Not much. LinkedIn can do what devOutpost does. The biggest difference is in the business model. LinkedIn renenue mostly comes from recruiters paying to be on their platform. Because of this, LinkedIn has no incentive to make it easier for contractors and potential clients to find each other. The other big difference is that devOutpost aims for quality over quantity. Without any recommendation, you will not find any new contract via devOutpost.

What is your business model?

At the moment, there is none. devOutpost doesn't make any money. Our mission is to make it easier for contractors and clients to find each other. If you have found this site to be useful and want to thank us? Please write us a testimonial and/or donate some money to charity.
